From logistics and retail to web development: what I kept from each role

Before building websites full-time, I worked in sales, fulfillment, logistics, and food service. Those roles built habits I now use daily as a frontend developer.

Customer clarity always wins

Customer-facing work taught me that confusion kills momentum. On websites, that means clear headlines, direct service descriptions, and obvious calls to action.

Organization is a technical skill

From fulfillment and inventory roles, I learned to build repeatable systems:

  • clear project scopes
  • organized component structures
  • predictable page templates
  • launch checklists that reduce mistakes

Communication matters as much as code

Sales and service roles taught me to ask better questions. Better questions lead to better requirements, smoother builds, and fewer revision cycles.

Technical + practical mindset

I use HTML, CSS, JavaScript, TypeScript, and Next.js to build modern websites, but reliability came from real jobs where speed and consistency were non-negotiable.
